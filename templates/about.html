Artificial Intelligence & Rehabilitation Robotics Intern:
During my four month placement I designed and ran novel experiments for contextual learning on a myoelectric prosthetic---or, powered artificial limb. I designed and implemented control system for use on both amputees and able-bodied subjects. Using contextual learning, the arm was able to learn about the user in real-time as they were interacting with their prosthetic: it was able to adapt predictions to new situations while maintaining knowledge of previous tasks.

During my four month placement I extended the BENTO Arm---a bionic limb for amputees. I created a simplified system for people of varying programming levels to implement machine learning experiments in real-time, enabling the prosthetic to learn about its wearer in real-time through interaction with their environment.

High School Intern:
For two years I participated in the Highschool Internship Programme at the University of Alberta: a six-week research placement in the Reinforcement Learning and Artificial Intelligence lab.During my first internship I and another intern devised an experiment to explore how having RL agent's learn from each other's actions improved learning. During my second internship I independently built a background subtraction program for the and made stand-alone applications for demonstrations during lab tours. I gave presentations for people of varying background knowledge in computing.
